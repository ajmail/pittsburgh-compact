Reform Framework

What We're Demanding

The Pittsburgh Compact

75% direct-to-founder disbursement mandate for all state innovation appropriations.

Intermediaries can continue to exist. They can charge fees. They can provide services. But public dollars must flow primarily to the entrepreneurs building companies, not the organizations claiming to support them.

The Standard

For every state innovation dollar appropriated:

  • Minimum 75¢ goes directly to founders as grants, investments, or loans
  • Maximum 25¢ funds intermediary operations, overhead, and services

This is not an elimination of intermediaries. It is a rebalancing toward efficient capital deployment in an era where advisory services are commoditized and direct capital is scarce.

Why 75%?

The 75% threshold is derived from empirical analysis and best practices:

  • Current state: Pennsylvania (25¢), Ohio (21¢) — intermediary capture is severe
  • Historical benchmark: Pennsylvania itself was at 42¢ in FY2023
  • Operational efficiency: Modern intermediaries require far less overhead in an AI era
  • Catalytic impact: Direct capital is what attracts private follow-on investment

75% is ambitious but achievable. It resets the default from "intermediary first" to "founder first."

Catalytic Capital Theory

Efficient state innovation funding does not substitute for private capital — it attracts it. A 75% direct-to-founder standard ensures public dollars act as:

  • Quality signal: State backing indicates institutional due diligence has been performed
  • First-loss de-risking: Public capital absorbs early-stage risk, making private follow-on more attractive
  • Market catalyst: Direct capital accelerates product development and traction, shortening time to private fundraising milestones

When state dollars flow directly to founders, they create momentum. When they get trapped in intermediary overhead, they create stagnation.

The Ask

Adopt the 75% direct-to-founder standard as a condition of Governor Shapiro's proposed $100 million "Innovate in PA 2.0" appropriation.

Make Pennsylvania a national model for efficient, accountable innovation funding in the AI era.

Let the forensic data — not politics — drive the policy.
